Mount Olympus

Mount Olympus, Greece’s tallest mountain and one of its most scenic sights, is located on the boundary between Thessaly and Macedonia, with its highest peak, Mytikas, reaching about 10,000 feet (2,918 m).

Mythology

Mount Olympus was the home of the gods and the location of Zeus’ throne in Greek mythology. In Greece and Asia Minor, the name Olympus was applied to a number of different summits, as well as hills, communities, and mythological figures.

Home to the Olympians

Because of its majesty, remoteness, and beauty, the Ancient Greeks have long believed that it is also the residence of their most significant gods. The twelve major gods, known as the Olympians, ruled the world from Olympus.

The Gods’ Time-Passing on Olympus

The gods feasted on ambrosia, drank nectar, and danced to the music of Apollo’s lyre on Olympus; obviously, they quarreled from time to time, primarily over the fortunes of mortal people. Mount Olympus acquired a far more legendary idea over time, frequently alluding to the unreachable heavenly area lying above the peaks themselves, rather than the real mountain.
