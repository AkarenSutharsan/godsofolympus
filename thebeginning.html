The Birth of Olympian Gods and Goddesses

In Greek mythology, themes of parental betrayal contrast with tales of filial betrayal are found about the early world. There’s also affection and loyalty to be found. They are all of the necessary elements of a solid plotline. Birth and the creation of the universe are inextricably interwoven. Mountains and other physical features of the planet are created via the process of procreation. Granted, it’s procreation between entities we don’t believe can procreate, but this is an ancient version and part of the ancient mythological worldview.

Generation Issues

  1. Parental Treachery: In Generation 1, Uranus (the sky), who doesn’t seem to have any love towards his offspring (or maybe he just wants his wife all to himself), hides his children inside his wife, Mother Earth (Gaia).
  2. Filial Betrayal: In Generation 2, Cronus (the titan father) swallows his children, which are the newborn Olympians.
  3. Parental Treachery: In Generation 3, the Olympic gods and goddesses have not learned from the examples of their ancestors, so there is more parental treachery and they don’t care for their children (the demigods), so they are left to suffer Alexander survive without them

Generation 1

First, before having a sexual partner, mother Gaia gave birth is Uranus (the sky). Once he grew up, he severed his father’s head, which allowed mother Gaia to give birth to her children.

The children born in this generation were...

  • the 50-headed Hecatonchires
  • the Cyclopes (Cyclops)
  • the 12 Titans

Generation 2

Before the 2nd generation of Titans were born, Uranus, who didn’t want to have children (because he was hateful and somewhat scared that one of his children might overthrow him) shut all his children inside his wife (mother Gaia), just like how her previous husband did.

But Gaia was angry with him, as Uranus threw her first son (Cyclopes) down to Tartarus, and she didn’t want any more of her children to be stuck down there, so she had her son, Cronos, to do the dirty work. Cronos, being the dutiful son, used a sickle and castrated his father, leaving him powerless.

After that Gaia’s children were released, rivers and springs, second-generation Titans, Atlas and Prometheus, moon (Selene), sun (Helios), and many others were brought to the world.

The children born in this generation were...

  • Cronus and Rhea
  • Iapetus and Themis
  • Oceanus and Tethys
  • Hyperion and Theia
  • Crius and Mnemosyne
  • Coeus and Phoebe

Generation 3

Cronus, the Titan, was terrified of his own children due to his father’s curse (Uranus). He was well aware of his father’s aggressive behavior. Cronus swallowed his offspring instead of imprisoning them in his wife’s body (or Tartarus) since he knew better than to repeat his father’s error of leaving himself exposed.

But just like her mother Gaia, Rhea also wanted her children to be free. So when Zeus was ready to be born, she gave him birth in secret. Zeus grew up safely until he was old enough to make his father regurgitate his five siblings (Hades, Poseidon, Demeter, Hera, and Hestia). Zeus then became the new leader/father since the 5 siblings grew up in their father’s stomach.

After that Gaia’s children were released, rivers and springs, second-generation Titans, Atlas and Prometheus, moon (Selene), sun (Helios), and many others were brought to the world.

The children born in this generation were...

  • Hestia
  • Hera
  • Demeter
  • Poseidon
  • Hades
  • Zeus

Generation 4

Zeus, being the first generation Olympian, chose to mate with his sister, Hera, and was the father to the following second generation.

The children born in this generation were...

  • Athena
  • Aphrodite
  • Ares
  • Apollo
  • Artemis
  • Dionysus
  • Hermes
  • Hephaestus
  • Persephone
